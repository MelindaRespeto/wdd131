Site Plan: Mayon Volcano

Site Purpose

This website aims to provide accessible and reliable information about Mayon Volcano, including its natural features, cultural significance, tourism highlights, and safety guidelines.

This site serves as a helpful resource for students, travelers, and anyone interested in learning about one of the Philippinesâ€™ most iconic volcanic landmarks. Located in the Bicol Region, it is part of the Pacific Ring of Fire. It is one of the most active volcanoes in the country, with over 50 eruptions recorded since 1616.

Despite its destructive potential, Mayon is also a popular tourist destination and a cultural symbol of the region. It attracts visitors for its scenic beauty and activities like ATV tours.

Color Schema / Palette

The site utilizes a **Green and Gray** color palette, defined in the CSS comments, for branding and to convey an image of nature, safety, and reliability.

Color Hex Value Application
Primary Green #10E86D / #2ABC6E Primary calls-to-action (buttons), navigation hover states, and key success indicators.
Secondary Gray #7F8281 / #666666 Footer background, secondary borders, link focus states, and standard body text for high readability.

Typography

The site utilizes the **Roboto** and **Open Sans** font families, adhering to a clear hierarchy for accessibility and professional presentation.

Element Font Family Weight Application
Primary Headings (H1, H2) Roboto 700 (Bold) Used for main page titles and primary section headings for strong visual impact.
Secondary Headings (H3, H4) Open Sans 600 (Semi-Bold) Used for sub-sections, captions, and article summaries.
Body & Navigation Open Sans 400 (Regular) Used for all long-form text, paragraphs, list items, and main navigation links for optimal readability.
