Site Plan: Mayon Volcano

Site Purpose

This website provides accessible and reliable information about Mayon Volcano, including its natural features, cultural significance, tourism highlights, and safety guidelines.

This site is a helpful resource for students, travelers, and anyone interested in learning about one of the Philippinesâ€™ most iconic volcanic landmarks. Located in the Bicol Region, it is part of the Pacific Ring of Fire and one of the most active volcanoes in the country, with over 50 eruptions recorded since 1616.

Mayon is a popular tourist destination and a cultural symbol of the region, attracting visitors for its scenic beauty and activities like ATV tours.

Color Schema / Palette

The site uses a Green and Gray color palette for branding, reflecting nature, safety, and reliability.

Color Hex Value Application
Primary Green #10E86D / #2ABC6E Used in buttons, navigation hover states, and success indicators.
Secondary Gray #7F8281 / #666666 Used in footer background, borders, link focus states, and body text.

Typography

The site uses Roboto and Open Sans font families with a clear hierarchy for readability and professional presentation.

Element Font Family Weight Application
Primary Headings (H1, H2) Roboto 700 (Bold) Used for main titles and section headings.
Secondary Headings (H3, H4) Open Sans 600 (Semi-Bold) Used for sub-sections, captions, and summaries.
Body & Navigation Open Sans 400 (Regular) Used for paragraphs, list items, and main navigation links.
